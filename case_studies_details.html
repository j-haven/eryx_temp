How TrackLine Logistics Replaced 14 Spreadsheets With One Live Dashboard

A regional freight network was losing hours every week reconciling fleet and warehouse numbers across disconnected files. Ninety days after go-live, every depot manager was reading from the same live board.

The Challenge

TrackLine's 42 depots each kept their own version of the weekly volume report. By the time regional leads compared notes on a Friday call, three different totals were on the table — and nobody could say which one reflected that morning's dispatch numbers.

Warehouse throughput, driver hours, and delivery exceptions lived in separate systems with no shared definitions, so even simple questions like "are we behind this week" took a full day of manual reconciliation to answer with any confidence.

02

The Approach

We connected TrackLine's dispatch system, warehouse scanners, and driver app directly into a single live layer, replacing manual exports with scheduled, automatic syncs. Every depot was mapped to shared definitions for volume, exceptions, and on-time rate before a single dashboard was built.

Rather than replacing every regional habit at once, we mirrored the Friday call format managers already trusted — just fed by one live number instead of five spreadsheets.

Rollout ran depot-by-depot over three weeks, with the old spreadsheets kept live in parallel until each region's numbers matched the new dashboard for two consecutive weeks running.

03

The Results

Within six weeks, all 42 depots were reporting from the same live board. Regional leads walked into Friday calls already aligned on the numbers, spending that time on exceptions and decisions instead of reconciliation.

Depot managers now save an average of 6.5 hours a week that previously went into building and cross-checking reports, and leadership's average turnaround on volume-related decisions dropped from nine days to three.

We used to spend the first twenty minutes of every regional call agreeing on whose numbers were right. Now we spend that time actually running the business.

Daniel Otieno Regional Operations Director, TrackLine Logistics
